\(\textit{1. What is a Copula?}\)
  • A copula is a function, with the marginal distributions of random variables as its inputs, and joint distribution of the random variables as its output.
  • Given a set of random variables with known marginal distributions, a copula can be used to model their joint distribution.
\(\textbf{\textit{Def 1.1 (Copula): }}\) Let \(X\) and \(Y\) be two random variables with marginal distributions \(F_X\) and \(F_Y\), and joint distribution \(F_{X, Y}\). Then the copula of \(X\) and \(Y\) is a function \(C_{X, Y}: [0, 1]^2 \to [0, 1]\) such that \[F_{X, Y}(x, y) = C_{X, Y}(F_X(x), F_Y(y))\text{.}\]
