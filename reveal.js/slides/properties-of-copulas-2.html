• If \(X\) and \(Y\) are continuous and independent, then
    1. 2.6. \(C_{X, Y}(u, v) = F_{X, Y}(x, y) = u v\)
  • This is the product copula, denoted by \(C_{X, Y}(u, v) = \Pi\).
  • If \(X\) and \(Y\) are continuous random variables, and \(\alpha\) and \(\beta\) are strictly increasing functions on the support of \(X\) and \(Y\), then
    1. 2.7. \(C_{\alpha(X), \beta(Y)}(u, v) = C_{X, Y}(u, v)\)
  • This is known as the invariance property of copulas (under strictly increasing transformations).
