• The most famous (or infamous) copula, obtained using the inversion method, is the Gaussian copula. We construct it as below:
  • Let \(X, Y\) be a bivariate normal random vector with \(\mu_X = \mu_Y = 0\), \(\sigma_X = \sigma_Y = 1\), and correlation coefficient \(\rho\). Then, the joint density function is given by: \[ f_{X, Y}(x, y) = \frac{1}{2\pi\sqrt{1 - \rho^2}} \exp\left(-\frac{1}{2(1 - \rho^2)}\left(x^2 - 2\rho xy + y^2\right)\right)\text{.} \] We also know, that \(X\) and \(Y\) are marginally standard normal random variables, \[ \begin{align*} f_X(x) &= \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} \exp\left(-\frac{x^2}{2}\right)\text{, } f_Y(y) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} \exp\left(-\frac{y^2}{2}\right)\text{.} \end{align*} \] Then, \[ \begin{align*} F_{X, Y}(x, y) &= \int_{-\infty}^x \int_{-\infty}^y f_{X, Y}(u, v) \, dv \, du \\ \implies F_{X, Y}(\Phi^{-1}(u), \Phi^{-1}(v)) &= \int_{-\infty}^{\Phi^{-1}(u)} \int_{-\infty}^{\Phi^{-1}(v)} f_{X, Y}(s, t) \, ds \, dt \tag{3.3} \text{,} \end{align*} \] where \(u = \Phi(x), v = \Phi(y)\) and \(\Phi^{-1}\) is the inverse of the standard normal cumulative distribution function.
