\(\textit{3. Constructing Copulas}\)
  • A common method for constructing copulas is known as the inversion method. Manipulating the definition of a coupla, \[ \begin{align*} F_{X, Y}(x, y) &= C_{X, Y}(F_X(x),F_Y(y)) \\ \implies C_{X, Y}(x, y) &= F_{X, Y}(F_X^{(-1)}(x), F_Y^{(-1)}(y)) \tag{3.1} \end{align*} \] Then, \[\begin{align*} {F'}_{X, Y}(x, y) &= C_{X, Y}({F'}_X(x), {F'}_Y(y)) \tag{3.2} \\ \end{align*}\]
  • (3.1) implies that a known joint distribution with known marginal distributions can be used to obtain a copula. Having the copula, (3.2) gives a way of obtaining different joint distributions with arbitrary marginals.
