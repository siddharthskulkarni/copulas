• Combining (3.1) and (3.3), we obtain \[ \begin{align*} C^{Gau}_{\rho}(u, v) &= F_{X, Y}(\Phi^{-1}(u), \Phi^{-1}(v)) = \int_{-\infty}^{\Phi^{-1}(u)} \int_{-\infty}^{\Phi^{-1}(v)} f_{X, Y}(s, t) \, ds \, dt \text{.} \end{align*} \]
  • This \(C^{Gau}_{\rho}(u, v)\) is the Gaussian copula with the correlation coefficient \(\rho\) as a parameter.
  • Plugging in arbitrary marginals to \(C^{Gau}_{\rho}(u, v)\) yields different joint distributions modelled by the Gaussian copula.
  • Li's copula, used to model the joint default times of bonds, is the Gaussian copula with exponential marginals.
