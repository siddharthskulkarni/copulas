\(\textit{4. Archimedean Copulas}\)
  • An important class of copulas, due to their ease of construction, simple properties and variety, are Archimedean copulas.
  • Common examples include the Clayton, Gumbel, and Frank copulas.
\(\textbf{\textit{Def 4.1 (Archimedean Copula): }}\) A copula \(C\) is Archimedean if there exists a generator function \(\phi: [0, 1] \to [0, \infty]\) with pseudo-inverse \(\phi^{-1}\) such that for all \(u, v \in [0, 1]\) \[ C(u, v) = \phi^{-1}(\phi(u) + \phi(v)) \text{.}\]
  • Often these copulas depend on a parameter \(\theta\) and are denoted as \(C_\theta\).
  • The Gumbel copula is defined as \[ C_\theta(u, v) = \exp\left(-\left[(-\ln u)^\theta + (-\ln v)^\theta\right]^{1/\theta}\right) \text{,}\] where \(\theta \geq 1\), and \(\phi(t) = (-\ln t)^\theta\) is the generator function.
