\(\textbf{\textit{Def 5.3 (Quadrant Dependence): }}\) Let \(X\) and \(Y\) be random variables. \(X\) and \( Y\) are said to be positively quadrant dependent (PQD) if \[P(X \leq x, Y \leq y) \geq P(X \leq x)P(Y \leq y)\text{, for all }x, y\text{.}\] \(X\) and \(Y\) are said to be negatively quadrant dependent (NQD) if \[P(X \leq x, Y \leq y) \leq P(X \leq x)P(Y \leq y)\text{, for all }x, y\text{.}\]
  • Tail dependence measures how likely it is for two dependent random variables to take extreme values \(\textit{together}\). Caring more about downsides, often our interest lies in the lower values, i.e left tail.
\(\textbf{\textit{Def 5.4 (Tail Dependence): }}\) Let \((X, Y)\) be a bivariate random vector with joint distribution \(F_{X, Y}\) and copula \(C_{X, Y}\). Then the coefficient of lower tail dependence denoted by \(\lambda_l\) is defined as \[\lambda_l = \lim_{q \to 0} P(Y \leq F_Y^{-1}(q) | X \leq F_X^{-1}(q))\text{.}\]
